## Typefaces and fonts

**[Typeface](../terms/typeface.md)**

A typeface is the overall design of a set of letters, numbers, and symbols that share a common visual style.

**[Font](../terms/font.md)**

A font is a specific style and weight of a typeface, today usually delivered as a digital file.

**[Font weight](../terms/font-weight.md)**

Font weight is the thickness of a typeface's strokes, ranging from very thin to very heavy.

**[Variable font](../terms/variable-font.md)**

A variable font is a single font file that can produce many styles by adjusting continuous sliders called axes.

## Anatomy and vertical metrics

**[Baseline](../terms/baseline.md)**

The baseline is the invisible line that most letters sit on.

**[Cap height](../terms/cap-height.md)**

Cap height is the distance from the baseline to the top of the uppercase letters.

**[x-height](../terms/x-height.md)**

The x-height is the height of a typeface's lowercase letters without ascenders or descenders.

**[Ascender](../terms/ascender.md)**

An ascender is the part of a lowercase letter that rises above the x-height.

**[Descender](../terms/descender.md)**

A descender is the part of a letter that drops below the baseline.

## Spacing

**[Kerning](../terms/kerning.md)**

Kerning is the adjustment of space between two specific letters so their pairing looks evenly balanced.

**[Tracking](../terms/tracking.md)**

Tracking is uniform spacing applied evenly across a run of letters, words, or a whole block of text.

**[Letter spacing](../terms/letter-spacing.md)**

Letter spacing is the design-tool and CSS control that adds or removes uniform space between every character in a run of text.

**[Leading](../terms/leading.md)**

Leading is the vertical space between lines of text, measured from one baseline to the next.

**[Line height](../terms/line-height.md)**

Line height is the property in CSS and design tools that sets the total vertical space a line of text occupies, including the text itself.

**[Vertical trim](../terms/vertical-trim.md)**

Vertical trim removes the extra space a font reserves above and below its letters so text aligns to cap height and baseline.

## Line and layout

**[Measure](../terms/measure.md)**

Measure is the length of a line of text, usually counted in characters per line.

## Style and detail

**[Small caps](../terms/small-caps.md)**

Small caps are uppercase letter shapes drawn at roughly the height of lowercase letters.

**[Figure styles](../terms/figure-styles.md)**

Figure styles are the different designs a font offers for numerals, such as lining vs oldstyle and tabular vs proportional.

**[Faux bold](../terms/faux-bold.md)**

Faux bold is fake boldness made by thickening a regular font's strokes instead of using a real bold design.

**[Faux italic](../terms/faux-italic.md)**

Faux italic is a fake slant created by tilting a regular font's glyphs rather than using a real italic design.

## Font technology and coverage

**[Font subsetting](../terms/font-subsetting.md)**

Font subsetting reduces a font file's size by keeping only the characters a page actually needs.

**[Fallback font](../terms/fallback-font.md)**

A fallback font is the next font a system uses when the preferred one is unavailable or missing a needed character.

**[Font coverage](../terms/font-coverage.md)**

Font coverage is the set of characters and languages a font can actually render.

## East Asian setting

**[Full-width](../terms/full-width.md)**

Full-width characters occupy one full em square, the same width as a typical CJK character, unlike narrower half-width forms.

**[Tategaki](../terms/tategaki.md)**

Tategaki is vertical writing, set top to bottom with columns running right to left, traditional in Japanese and other East Asian scripts.
