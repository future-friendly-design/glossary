# Bengali-Assamese

## Definition

Bengali-Assamese is the Brahmic abugida used to write Bengali, Assamese, and several related eastern South Asian languages.

## Why it matters

Also called Eastern Nagari, the Bengali-Assamese script is an eastern [Brahmic](brahmic-scripts.md) [abugida](../language-terms/writing-systems-and-scripts/abugida.md). Consonants carry an inherent vowel modified by [matra](../language-terms/writing-systems-and-scripts/matra.md)s, clusters combine into [conjunct](../language-terms/writing-systems-and-scripts/conjunct.md) forms, and it is written left to right. It writes Bengali and Assamese, two of India's official languages, and is the national script of Bangladesh, so it serves a very large readership and brings the same complex-text-layout needs as [Devanagari](devanagari.md) while differing in letterforms. Assamese uses a few letterform differences from Bengali, mainly in the characters for "r" and "w/v".

## Example

Bengali and Assamese use almost the same letters, differing mainly in the characters for "r" and "w/v".
