## Definition
Bengali-Assamese is the Brahmic abugida used to write Bengali, Assamese, and several related eastern South Asian languages.

## Why it matters
Also called Eastern Nagari, the Bengali-Assamese script is an eastern [[brahmic-scripts|Brahmic]] [[abugida]]. Consonants carry an inherent vowel modified by [[matra]]s, clusters combine into [[conjunct]] forms, and it is written left to right. It writes Bengali and Assamese, two of India's official languages, and is the national script of Bangladesh, so it serves a very large readership and brings the same complex-text-layout needs as [[devanagari|Devanagari]] while differing in letterforms. Assamese uses a few letterform differences from Bengali, mainly in the characters for "r" and "w/v".

## Example
Bengali and Assamese use almost the same letters, differing mainly in the characters for "r" and "w/v".
