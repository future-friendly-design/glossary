## Definition
Devanagari is the Brahmic abugida used to write Hindi, Sanskrit, Marathi, Nepali, and other South Asian languages.

## Why it matters
Devanagari is an [abugida](abugida.md) in the [Brahmic family](brahmic-scripts.md), written left to right with a distinctive horizontal headline ([shirorekha](shirorekha.md)) running along the top of the letters. Each consonant carries an inherent vowel changed by adding vowel signs ([matra](matra.md)s), and consonant clusters combine into [conjunct](conjunct.md) forms. For interface work it is a frequent first encounter with real complex text layout: a left-side matra typed after its consonant displays before it, the shirorekha must join cleanly across a cluster, and conjuncts have to form. It is one of the most widely used scripts in South Asia.

## Example
The consonant "k" carries an inherent "a"; adding a matra turns it into "ki" or "ku". When a left-side i-matra is added, it is typed after the consonant but rendered to its left.
