## Definition
Ethnologue is a comprehensive reference catalogue of the world's living languages, with data on speakers, locations, and classification.

## Why it matters
First published in 1951 and maintained by SIL Global, Ethnologue catalogues over 7,000 living languages with speaker numbers, geographic distribution, and genetic classification, organized by world area and country. It is a primary source of ISO 639-3 language codes and is widely cited in linguistics and language technology. Worth knowing before you rely on it: since 2019 most of its content sits behind a paywall, with free or discounted access for users in lower-income countries and some researchers, so [[glottolog]] is often used as an open complement.

## Example
Designers checking how many people use a given language can look up its Ethnologue entry for speaker estimates.
