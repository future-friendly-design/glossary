# Exonym

## Definition
An exonym is the name outsiders use for a language, place, or people, different from the name that group uses for itself.

## Why it matters
An exonym is the flip side of an [autonym](autonym.md): the name given from outside, rather than the name a group uses for itself. English speakers say "German," "Finnish," and "Japanese"; the speakers themselves say "Deutsch," "suomi," and "日本語." For interface work the practical rule is the one the autonym entry makes: a language picker should show each language by its autonym, not its English exonym, so a user spots their language at a glance. It also matters because some exonyms are dated or politically loaded, and a community may prefer not to be labeled by one, so treat the choice of name as a respect decision sourced from [CLDR](cldr.md), not a default.

## Example
"German" is the exonym English speakers use for the language its own speakers call "Deutsch."
