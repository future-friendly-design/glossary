## Definition
A lexifier is the language that supplies most of the vocabulary for a pidgin or creole.

## Why it matters
When a contact language forms, one language (often that of a dominant or colonial group) provides the bulk of the words, while grammar may come from other "substrate" languages or develop anew. That word-supplying language is the lexifier. Many [[creole]]s are named after their lexifier, such as French-based or English-based creoles, which is a useful shorthand but says nothing about a creole's grammar or its status as a full language in its own right.

## Example
French is the lexifier of Haitian Creole, which draws most of its vocabulary from French.
