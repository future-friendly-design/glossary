## Definition
A macrolanguage is an ISO 639-3 code that covers several closely related individual languages treated as one language identity in some contexts.

## Why it matters
In [[iso-639-3|ISO 639-3]], a macrolanguage groups individual languages that are very closely related and that share a single common language identity in at least one setting, such as a shared written standard. This lets a broad label coexist with the finer-grained codes for its member languages. It matters for software because the level you tag content at changes behavior: labelling something "Arabic" is coarser than naming a specific variety, and a language picker, font choice, or locale may need the macrolanguage in one place and the individual language in another.

## Example
"Arabic" (ara) is a macrolanguage that includes individual varieties such as Egyptian Arabic (arz) and Moroccan Arabic (ary); ISO 639-3 lists around 28 individual languages under it, a count that shifts between releases.
