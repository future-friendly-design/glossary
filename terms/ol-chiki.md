# Ol Chiki

## Definition

Ol Chiki is the alphabet created in 1925 by Pandit Raghunath Murmu for the Santali language of eastern India.

## Why it matters

Ol Chiki is a 30-letter [alphabet](alphabet.md) devised by Pandit Raghunath Murmu to write Santali, a Munda (Austroasiatic) language that is one of the scheduled languages of India. Rather than adapt the [Brahmic abugidas](brahmic-scripts.md) common in the region, it is a true alphabet that gives vowels their own full letters alongside consonants, written left to right. For product and type teams it is a working example of a purpose-built community script with a real and growing digital footprint: it was encoded in Unicode in version 5.1 (2008) in the block U+1C50 to U+1C7F, so fonts, keyboards, and rendering for it are a concrete deliverable, not a special case to skip.

## Example

Ol Chiki writes each vowel as its own full letter, rather than as a mark attached to a consonant.
