## Definition
Reference sources are the authoritative catalogues, standards, and font projects this glossary relies on to ground facts about languages, scripts, and text.

## Why it matters
Decisions about language support are only as trustworthy as the data behind them. When you need to know how many people speak a language, how it is classified, whether it is endangered, what the character standard actually says, or which free fonts cover a script, guessing is how products end up excluding people. This category collects the places worth trusting for those answers, so claims in design and engineering can be checked against a source rather than assumed.
