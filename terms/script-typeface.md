# Script (typeface)

## Definition
A script typeface is a typeface classification for designs that imitate handwriting or calligraphy, usually with letters that connect or flow into one another.

## Why it matters
"Script" is polysemous. In the writing-systems sense it means a set of signs (see [script](script.md)); in computing it means a program; and in type design it is a classification of [typeface](typeface.md)s, the sense this entry covers. A script typeface is the handwriting-derived category that sits alongside serif, sans serif, slab serif, and display in the way designers sort typefaces by form. Its defining trait is that it evokes a pen or brush, and in many script faces the letters are drawn to join, so it carries a different feel and different practical limits from a workhorse text face. Naming it as its own category keeps the three meanings of "script" from colliding when designers and engineers talk to each other.

## Example
A wedding-invitation font with looping, connected letters is a script typeface; Helvetica, a sans serif, and Times, a serif, are not.
