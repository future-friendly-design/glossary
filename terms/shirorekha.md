# Shirorekha

## Definition

The shirorekha is the horizontal headline that runs along the top of letters in Devanagari and related scripts.

## Why it matters

In scripts such as Devanagari and Bengali, most characters hang from a horizontal top line that visually ties the letters of a word together. When consonants form [conjunct](conjunct.md)s or half-forms, the shirorekha has to join continuously across the cluster, which a font must render seamlessly or the word looks broken. It is sometimes called the matra line, which is not to be confused with a [matra](../language-terms/writing-systems-and-scripts/matra.md) vowel sign.

## Example

In Devanagari "नमस्ते", the unbroken line across the top of the word is the shirorekha.
