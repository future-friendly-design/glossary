## Definition
A syllabary is a script where each symbol stands for a whole syllable rather than a single sound.

## Why it matters
In a syllabary each character usually represents a consonant-plus-vowel unit, and the symbols for different syllables are not systematically related to one another. This is what distinguishes a syllabary from an [abugida](abugida.md), where syllables that share a consonant also share a base shape. Japanese hiragana and katakana are syllabaries, as is Cherokee. Recognizing the category helps explain why these scripts have a fixed, learnable set of symbols rather than a small alphabet recombined into many sounds.

## Example
The Japanese hiragana symbols ka, ki, ku, ke, ko are five separate characters with no shared base shape.
