# Wancho script

## Definition

The Wancho script is an alphabet created in the 2000s by Banwang Losu for the Wancho language of Arunachal Pradesh, India.

## Why it matters

The Wancho script was developed between roughly 2001 and 2012 by Banwang Losu for Wancho, a Konyak (Sino-Tibetan) language of Longding district, Arunachal Pradesh. Its letters represent consonants and vowels, it does not use conjuncts, and tone is marked with [diacritic](../language-terms/writing-systems-and-scripts/diacritic.md)s on vowel letters. It was encoded in Unicode in version 12.0 (2019) in the block U+1E2C0 to U+1E2FF. It is a recent, fully encoded community script, and like its neighbors Wancho is also written in [Devanagari](devanagari.md) or [Latin](latin-script.md), so supporting the language can mean supporting more than one script.

## Example

In the Wancho script, tone is shown by adding a diacritic to a vowel letter.
